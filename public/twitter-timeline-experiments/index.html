The Twitter Noise Problem

Thu, Apr 30, 2015

How to follow pmarca on Twitter?

Many months ago I asked a group of people this question. @pmarca is the Twitter handle for Marc Andreessen, a prolific tweet and inventor of the Tweetstorm (among other things) [1]. He has lots of interesting to say, but he averages more than 100 tweets a day, which is not something I feel like keeping up with.

Common answers to this question are: (a) don’t try to be keep up, and (b) make judicious use of lists. While useful, I don’t think these suggestion hits the core of the problem:

A person tweeting 100 times a day gets equal exposure to someone tweeting once a day, even though you are equally interested in what both people have to say.

This is the noise problem. The obvious solution is to make your exposure to tweets proportional to your interest in them, but this is hardly as easy as it sounds.

Hypothesis

Filtering out half my tweets, either at random or by some tweet velocity metric, will make my Twitter browsing a more enjoyable experience.

EXPERIMENT

Levelfield REST API, OAuth 1 not getting working, many requests otherwise, too much hassle.

Relatif Streaming API, take a bunch of ids and get lots of data, max 5000, otherwise user API, unclear how to get working client-side, lots of work. Problem is with no favourite count, so can’t measure velocity.

CONCLUSION

Essentially these experiments were failures. Future directions.

Ill-specified: Another thing is read-only. Hypothesis came after, was seeing it more as a prototype so I was doing some interface work that didn’t end up being useful.

A lot of work to setup the other thing, so to get a reasonable feedback loop I ended up using pmarca’s Twitter stream, but these results I didn’t really care about. I also did some batching.

Too complicated?

Good case study in where motivation is low.

Lost momentum both cases.

Might try again.
